## Overview

Buying domains with cryptocurrency extends the payment options beyond USDT to include a broader range of digital assets. Each cryptocurrency presents distinct characteristics in terms of price stability, transaction speed, network fees, and payment gateway compatibility that domain holders must evaluate before initiating payment.

## Cryptocurrency Payment Comparison

| Cryptocurrency | Type | Volatility | Settlement | Typical Fee |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| USDT (ERC-20) | Stablecoin | Low | 1-3 min | $2-15 |
| USDT (TRC-20) | Stablecoin | Low | <1 min | $0.1-1 |
| USDC (ERC-20) | Stablecoin | Low | 1-3 min | $2-15 |
| BTC | Volatile | High | 10-60 min | $1-30 |
| ETH | Volatile | High | 1-5 min | $2-50 |
| SOL | Volatile | Medium | <1 min | $0.01-0.05 |

Stablecoin payments (USDT, USDC) offer predictable payment amounts, while volatile cryptocurrency payments (BTC, ETH) expose domain holders to exchange rate risk during the payment window.

## Payment Gateway Mechanisms

Cryptocurrency payment gateways operate as intermediaries that: generate payment invoices with unique wallet addresses, monitor blockchain for incoming transactions, convert received cryptocurrency to fiat at the prevailing exchange rate, and remit fiat payment to the registrar.

The gateway model introduces conversion fees (typically 1-3%), payment timeout windows (15-30 minutes), and exchange rate slippage risk for volatile cryptocurrency payments.

## Compliance Boundaries

This site presents cryptocurrency domain payment processes for educational and research purposes. Content must not be used for regulatory evasion, sanction circumvention, or other illegal purposes. Using cryptocurrency to pay for domain registration does not alter applicable KYC requirements or sanctions screening obligations.
