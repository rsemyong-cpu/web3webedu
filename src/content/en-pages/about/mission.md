## Page Position

This page explains why this site researches crypto payments, domain infrastructure, Web3 identity, and the stablecoin economy. As the domain industry intersects with cryptocurrency payment methods, stablecoin settlement infrastructure, and evolving privacy regulations, independent research becomes essential for domain holders, startup teams, and technical professionals seeking reliable, up-to-date reference material. Our research scope covers registrar crypto payment integration, DNS security developments, stablecoin payment infrastructure maturity, and the emerging Web3 identity layer.

## Editorial Principles

This site adheres to research, education, reference library, and tool-based expression. We do not provide financial investment advice, facilitate illegal anonymous services, or offer guidance for regulatory evasion, sanction circumvention, money laundering, or fraud. All content is produced for educational and informational purposes. When discussing registrar policies, stablecoin payment methods, DNS security vulnerabilities, or privacy protection services, we apply conservative language and clearly state the limitations of our observations. Readers should consult registrars' official terms, applicable laws, and qualified professionals before making specific decisions.

## Content Review

All core pages should be checked for title, description, URL, FAQ, risk disclosure, internal links, references, and update timestamp. Topics involving registrar policies, stablecoin payments, DNS security, or private registration require conservative language with stated limitations. Our review process includes verifying that external references remain accessible and accurate, that internal links point to the correct English-language paths, and that risk disclosures are present wherever payment methods or security topics are discussed. Update timestamps reflect the most recent editorial review, not necessarily changes to the underlying subject matter.
